# Tech Investment Steering Committee — Minutes, Q1 2026 Meeting

## Attendance and Prior Action Items

The Q1 2026 quarterly meeting of the Copperline Retail Group Tech Investment Steering Committee was held on March 12, 2026. The meeting was co-chaired by Renata Alvarez (EVP & CFO) and Marcus Whitfield (SVP & CIO). Voting and participating members present were Sasha Kim (VP Digital Commerce), Devon Marsh (VP Supply Chain & Logistics), Farrukh Rahimi (VP & CISO), and Talia Novak (VP Customer Experience). Omar Delgado (Director, FP&A) attended as financial-model preparer and presenter of record for the business cases under review. Elena Popescu (Director, Enterprise PMO) attended as facilitator and scribe. A quorum was present for the full duration of the meeting.

The co-chairs opened by reviewing the status of prior-period action items carried into Q1. The post-implementation review of the Cloud Infrastructure Migration, directed at the December 2025 meeting, was confirmed complete: the twelve-month PIR was finalized in January 2026 and its findings were noted for the portfolio status discussion later in the agenda. The post-implementation review of the Omnichannel POS / Unified Commerce program, which went live in August 2025, was likewise confirmed complete as of February 2026, with its findings flagged for follow-up given an identified benefit shortfall. Popescu confirmed that both PIR documents had been circulated to committee members in advance of the meeting. No prior action items were left open or overdue entering the meeting.

Alvarez set the framing for the session. The primary business of the Q1 meeting was to consider two business cases that had completed PMO review under the Business Case Review Policy and were ready for a disposition decision: the Cybersecurity Modernization — Zero Trust program sponsored by Rahimi, and the Customer Data Platform and AI Personalization program sponsored by Kim. Both cases fell within the committee's approval authority, as neither carried a single-fiscal-year capital ask above the $5M threshold that would require ratification by the Board Finance & Audit Committee. The agenda then turned to each case in sequence, followed by a portfolio status update and the recording of new action items.

## Agenda Item — Cybersecurity Modernization Business Case

Rahimi presented the Cybersecurity Modernization — Zero Trust Network Access business case, built on the Sentrywall ZTNA platform, with Whitfield speaking to the case as executive sponsor and Delgado presenting the financial model. Rahimi framed the request as a response to a deteriorating rather than stable baseline: Copperline's perimeter-based security model, he argued, no longer reflects how the company operates across 214 stores, four distribution centers, a cloud-hosted workload base, and a permanently hybrid workforce of roughly 9,600 employees. He emphasized three drivers — the fragmentation of the attack surface following the 2025 cloud migration, the intensification of retail-sector ransomware and credential-theft activity, and tightening PCI DSS and cyber-insurance control expectations — and characterized the flat, implicitly trusted internal network as the company's most material unmitigated technology risk.

Delgado walked the committee through the financials: a three-year total cost of ownership of $10.4M, a projected three-year risk-adjusted benefit of $16.9M, a net present value of $5.1M at the standard 8% discount rate, and a payback period of 24 months. He drew the committee's attention to the deliberate decision not to compute an internal rate of return for this case, explaining that the dominant benefit stream is expected loss avoidance, which is probabilistic by nature, and that presenting an IRR against a risk-adjusted loss-avoidance figure would imply a precision the estimate does not support. He confirmed that NPV and payback were therefore the intended primary decision metrics.

Discussion was substantive. Alvarez pressed on benefit-realization risk, noting that a security investment whose value is loss avoidance is structurally hard to prove after the fact and asking how the committee would know, in eighteen months, whether the money had been well spent. Rahimi responded that the program would report against leading control indicators the team can directly move — reduction in standing privileged access, segmentation coverage of the cardholder data environment, MFA enforcement rates, and mean time to contain — and would anchor the harder-to-observe loss-avoidance claim to the two concrete and observable streams, compliance remediation cost and cyber-insurance renewal economics. Marsh raised the question of rollout risk across a heterogeneous store estate; Rahimi pointed to the two-phase structure, under which Phase 1 secures the highest-risk systems first and Phase 2 paces the fleet-wide extension and legacy VPN retirement to capacity. Kim asked about the interaction between the Zero Trust rollout and the customer-facing systems in her portfolio, and Rahimi confirmed that the Phase 1 scope centered on the cardholder data environment, finance and treasury, and privileged access, with customer-facing store systems addressed in the paced Phase 2.

Whitfield closed the discussion by underscoring that even under the case's pessimistic scenario the program remains NPV-positive and its compliance and insurance benefits continue to accrue, and that the cost of inaction rises as the threat and insurance environments move against the company. On the co-chairs' motion, the committee recorded a disposition of **approved** for the Cybersecurity Modernization — Zero Trust program at the $10.4M three-year TCO, with the program to proceed on its phased plan and to report through the standard portfolio benefits-tracking cadence. The committee directed that Phase 1 begin immediately and that Phase 2 planning proceed in parallel.

## Agenda Item — Customer Data Platform and AI Personalization Business Case

Kim presented the Customer Data Platform and AI Personalization business case, built on the Lumenpoint CDP platform, with Delgado again presenting the financial model. Kim framed the case as the connective tissue between the company's channels: with e-commerce now roughly 18% of a $1.92B revenue base and the Omnichannel POS program live since August 2025, Copperline holds increasingly rich but fragmented customer data across store, e-commerce, and loyalty systems, and lacks a unified customer profile capable of driving coordinated personalization across channels. She positioned the CDP as the foundation for closing that gap and for realizing the cross-channel value the POS investment was intended to unlock.

Delgado presented the financials: a three-year total cost of ownership of $7.1M, a projected three-year benefit of $11.8M, a net present value of $3.4M at the 8% discount rate, a payback period of 20 months, and an internal rate of return of 29%. He noted that, unlike the cybersecurity case, the CDP benefits are grounded in projectable revenue lift and marketing-efficiency gains, which supports a computed IRR.

The committee's discussion focused on benefit realization and sequencing. Alvarez, referencing the just-completed POS PIR, cautioned that the POS omnichannel revenue lift had come in materially below pace at its six-month review, root-caused substantially to training-completion gaps, and asked how the CDP case avoided repeating that pattern of optimistic adoption assumptions. Kim acknowledged the parallel directly and argued that the CDP's benefit case depends less on frontline behavioral change and more on back-end data unification and marketing execution, which she characterized as more controllable, while committing to build explicit adoption and enablement milestones into the delivery plan. Novak asked whether the CDP's personalization capabilities would create dependencies with the customer-service and experience systems in her portfolio; Kim confirmed the CDP was designed as a shared data foundation that would benefit those systems without creating a hard delivery dependency. Whitfield noted the technical alignment between the CDP and the cloud infrastructure the company migrated to in 2025, which he said reduced integration risk relative to an on-premise build.

On the co-chairs' motion, the committee recorded a disposition of **approved** for the Customer Data Platform and AI Personalization program at the $7.1M three-year TCO, targeting a go-live in Q1 2027. The committee attached an expectation, short of a formal condition, that the delivery plan incorporate explicit adoption and enablement milestones informed by the POS experience, and directed the PMO to track those milestones through the benefits cadence.

## Portfolio Status Update

Popescu presented a brief status update on the in-flight and recently completed portfolio. The Cloud Infrastructure Migration, approved in November 2024 and live since January 2025, completed its twelve-month post-implementation review in January 2026; the committee noted that opex-savings realization stood at 92% of the $7.1M annual target as of the PIR, an encouraging but not-yet-complete trajectory that the committee asked to see tracked forward through the benefits cadence. The Omnichannel POS / Unified Commerce program, live since August 2025, completed its six-month PIR in February 2026; the committee noted the revenue-lift shortfall — approximately 58% of pace — and its root-causing to training-completion gaps, and flagged the adoption lesson as directly relevant to the CDP program just approved.

The ERP Replacement program ("Summit" to Ledgerline Cloud ERP), approved and board-ratified in December 2024 and in flight, was reported as progressing through implementation. Alvarez, as the ERP sponsor, noted that the program remained within its approved envelope as of this meeting but that discovery activities were ongoing and would be reported at the next quarterly review. No decision was requested on the ERP program at this meeting.
