# Tech Investment Steering Committee — Minutes, Q3 2026 Meeting

## Attendance and Prior Action Items

The Q3 2026 quarterly meeting of the Copperline Retail Group Tech Investment Steering Committee was held on September 15, 2026. The meeting was co-chaired by Renata Alvarez (EVP & CFO) and Marcus Whitfield (SVP & CIO). Voting and participating members present were Sasha Kim (VP Digital Commerce), Devon Marsh (VP Supply Chain & Logistics), Farrukh Rahimi (VP & CISO), and Talia Novak (VP Customer Experience). Omar Delgado (Director, FP&A) attended as financial-model preparer and presenter of record for the two new business cases under review. Elena Popescu (Director, Enterprise PMO) attended as facilitator and scribe. A quorum was present throughout.

The co-chairs opened with a review of prior action items. The Cybersecurity ZTNA Phase 1 build directed at the Q1 meeting was confirmed complete and in production; Phase 2, the fleet-wide extension and legacy VPN retirement, was confirmed in progress and on plan. The CDP delivery plan with explicit adoption and enablement milestones was confirmed finalized, with the program tracking toward its Q1 2027 go-live target. The recurring Cloud Migration benefits-tracking item was confirmed live and folded into the benefits-tracking update later in the agenda. The POS training-completion remediation follow-up was confirmed underway. The one prior item carrying material new information was the ERP implementation and discovery status update, which the co-chairs elevated to the first substantive agenda item given the significance of what discovery had surfaced.

Alvarez framed the session. The Q3 meeting carried three decision-weight items: a briefing on a material ERP scope change and the governance response it required, and two new business cases ready for disposition — the WMS + Robotics Pilot sponsored by Marsh and the AI Customer Service Chatbot sponsored by Novak. She noted at the outset that the committee would be considering these two new investment requests against a FY2026 capital picture that had tightened since the start of the year, and asked the committee to hold that constraint in view through both cases.

## Agenda Item — ERP Replacement Scope Change Briefing

Popescu briefed the committee on the ERP Replacement program ("Summit" to Ledgerline Cloud ERP), which was approved and board-ratified in December 2024 and remains in flight. The original business case, prepared in October 2024, carried a three-year total cost of ownership of $22.5M, a projected three-year benefit of $29.7M, a payback period of 28 months, an NPV of $4.9M at the 8% discount rate, and an IRR of 19%. That case, Popescu reminded the committee, scoped six legacy integrations between the incumbent Summit environment and surrounding systems.

The core of the briefing was a discovery finding. As the implementation progressed into detailed integration mapping, the program identified 14 undocumented legacy integrations against the 6 that had been scoped in the original case — more than double the integration surface the budget and timeline were built on. Popescu explained that these integrations were not optional or deprecated connections but active data pathways carrying finance, inventory, and operational data that the ERP cutover cannot proceed without accounting for. The practical consequence is that both the implementation budget and the timeline as approved are no longer adequate, and the program requires a formal revision rather than absorption within the existing envelope.

Discussion was pointed. Alvarez, as ERP sponsor, characterized the finding as a serious but not surprising category of ERP risk — integration discovery is a well-known source of scope expansion — while acknowledging that the magnitude of the gap, a more than twofold increase in integration count, was material. Whitfield asked whether the discovery reflected a failure of the original scoping or an artifact of undocumented legacy complexity; Popescu attributed it substantially to the latter, noting that the undocumented integrations were precisely those that the incumbent environment's poor documentation had obscured, which is itself part of the rationale for replacing it. Marsh and Kim both noted the downstream implications for their own portfolios, given the ERP's role as the system of record their programs depend on.

The committee directed a formal governance response rather than an ad hoc adjustment. It directed Elena Popescu and the PMO to prepare a formal change-request addendum to the ERP business case, documenting the revised scope, a revised budget, and a revised timeline reflecting the 14-versus-6 integration finding. The committee was explicit that the revision would represent a material increase to the approved envelope, to be quantified in that forthcoming addendum, and noted that if the revised single-fiscal-year capital ask crossed the $5M ratification threshold, the addendum would require ratification by the Board Finance & Audit Committee under the standard governance framework. The disposition recorded for the original case pending that addendum was that it would be superseded-by-addendum once the revision is finalized. Popescu indicated the addendum would be prepared within roughly two weeks, targeting late September 2026.

## Agenda Item — WMS + Robotics Pilot Business Case

Marsh presented the WMS + Robotics Pilot business case, covering the replacement of the incumbent Trailhead WMS with Kinetic Flow WMS and the introduction of Orbis Robotics into the distribution-center network. Delgado presented the financial model: a three-year total cost of ownership of $12.8M, a projected three-year benefit of $17.3M, a payback period of 25 months, an NPV of $3.2M at the 8% discount rate, and an IRR of 22%. Marsh argued the case on labor productivity, throughput, and accuracy gains across the four distribution centers, and framed the robotics component as a pilot deliberately scoped to prove the model before any fleet-wide commitment.

The committee found the case sound on its own merits. Members did not dispute the operational logic or the financial projections, which compared favorably to the portfolio. The obstacle was capital timing rather than quality. Alvarez brought the FY2026 constraint to the foreground: with the ERP program's scope change poised to absorb a material increase in capital — the very increase the committee had just directed be quantified in an addendum — the FY2026 capital headroom that a $12.8M program would draw against was no longer available without displacing commitments already in flight, including ERP completion and Cybersecurity Phase 2. Marsh acknowledged the constraint and did not press for approval against it, noting that the pilot's value did not depend on starting in FY2026 specifically.

After discussion, on the co-chairs' motion, the committee recorded a disposition of **deferred** for the WMS + Robotics Pilot, deferring the program to FY2027 Q2 explicitly on the basis of FY2026 capital constraints driven by the ERP overrun rather than any deficiency in the case. The committee directed that the case be carried forward for reconsideration at the Q2 2027 meeting, refreshed for any changes in vendor pricing or DC operating conditions, and that the CFO's forthcoming FY2027 prioritization work reflect the deferral.

## Agenda Item — AI Customer Service Chatbot Business Case

Novak presented the AI Customer Service Chatbot business case, built on the Conversant AI platform, intended to automate a share of customer-service contact volume across the e-commerce and store-support channels. Delgado presented the financials: a three-year total cost of ownership of $4.3M, a projected three-year benefit of $6.9M, a payback period of 17 months, an NPV of $2.0M at the 8% discount rate, and an IRR of 33% — the highest headline IRR of any case in the current portfolio.

Despite the attractive headline returns, the committee's discussion surfaced substantive reservations. Alvarez and Whitfield both questioned the durability and risk profile of the benefit. The concern was not the arithmetic but the assumptions beneath it: the benefit case leaned heavily on containment and deflection rates that the committee viewed as optimistic given Copperline's recent experience with adoption-dependent benefits, most directly the POS omnichannel shortfall root-caused to training and adoption gaps. Novak defended the case but acknowledged that the deflection assumptions carried real uncertainty and that a customer-facing conversational system introduced brand and customer-experience risk if it underperformed. Kim noted a sequencing concern: the chatbot's value would be materially higher once the CDP had unified customer data, and launching it ahead of that foundation risked a weaker first version. Alvarez brought the same FY2026 capital constraint to bear, and observed that a discretionary, adoption-dependent investment was the weakest candidate for the limited headroom available.

After discussion, on the co-chairs' motion, the committee recorded a disposition of **rejected** for the AI Customer Service Chatbot for FY2027. The rejection rested on the combination of optimistic adoption-dependent benefit assumptions, sequencing ahead of the CDP foundation, brand and customer-experience risk, and the FY2026 capital constraint. The committee noted that rejection for FY2027 did not preclude a future resubmission on a stronger foundation, particularly once the CDP was live, but declined to carry the current case forward as an active or deferred item.

## Benefits Tracking Update

Popescu presented the recurring portfolio benefits-tracking update. The headline was the continued positive trajectory of the Cloud Infrastructure Migration opex savings: realization had reached approximately 97% of the $7.1M annual target as of this meeting, up from 95% at the Q2 2026 benefits tracker and 92% at the January 2026 twelve-month PIR. The committee noted the trend approvingly as evidence of a benefit case realizing substantially in full over time, and contrasted it with the POS omnichannel revenue-lift shortfall, where remediation of training-completion gaps remained in progress. The Cybersecurity ZTNA and CDP programs, both approved at Q1, were reported as tracking to plan, with the cybersecurity program reporting on its leading control indicators pending its eventual transition into post-implementation monitoring.
