# Memorandum: FY2027 Technology Capital Allocation and Portfolio Prioritization

## Purpose

This memorandum is issued by Renata Alvarez, EVP & CFO, on October 20, 2026, to the members of the Tech Investment Steering Committee and to the Board Finance & Audit Committee. Its purpose is to set out the recommended prioritization of Copperline Retail Group's technology investment portfolio for FY2027, ahead of the December Board budget approval, and to give the rationale for the capital-allocation choices that prioritization implies. It consolidates the dispositions taken across the Q1 and Q3 2026 Steering Committee meetings, the post-implementation and benefits-tracking evidence gathered through the year, and the material ERP scope change confirmed in September into a single view of where FY2027 technology capital should and should not go.

The memo is written against a specific constraint that shapes every recommendation in it: the ERP Replacement program's confirmed scope expansion will absorb a material share of FY2027 technology capital headroom, and the portfolio must be sequenced accordingly. This is not a memo about cutting the technology agenda; the company's revenue base of $1.92B, its roughly 18% e-commerce mix, and its 214-store, four-DC operating footprint all continue to demand sustained technology investment. It is a memo about doing first things first in a year in which not everything can be done at once.

First, the ERP figures in the table are the original-case numbers; the confirmed scope change means the effective FY2027 cost of that program will be higher than the original $22.5M three-year TCO implies, with the precise revision quantified in the change-request addendum that supersedes the original case. ## Capital Constraints and Rationale

The organizing fact of FY2027 is that the ERP program will cost materially more than approved. The September Steering Committee briefing confirmed that integration discovery had identified 14 undocumented legacy integrations against the 6 scoped in the original October 2024 case — more than double the integration surface the $22.5M budget and 28-month timeline were built on. These are active finance, inventory, and operational data pathways that the cutover cannot proceed without, so the additional cost is non-discretionary: it is the cost of finishing a program the company is already committed to and has already board-ratified. The change-request addendum being prepared by the PMO will quantify the revision, and to the extent the revised single-fiscal-year ask crosses the $5M threshold, it will come to the Board Finance & Audit Committee for ratification under our standard governance framework.

The consequence for the rest of the portfolio is arithmetic. ERP is a Tier 1 commitment that must be funded to completion; Cybersecurity Phase 2 is likewise a Tier 1 commitment whose deferral would leave the company exposed on exactly the compliance and insurance controls that are becoming preconditions rather than favorable factors; and the CDP is far enough along, and close enough to its Q1 2027 go-live, that stopping it would strand sunk investment and forfeit the cross-channel value it is meant to unlock. Once those commitments are funded, the FY2027 headroom that would have been available for genuinely new, discretionary programs is substantially consumed.

That is the direct rationale for the two dispositions the Steering Committee took in September. The WMS + Robotics Pilot was deferred to FY2027 Q2 not because the case is weak — at a $12.8M TCO, a $3.2M NPV, a 22% IRR, and a 25-month payback it is a sound program — but because a $12.8M discretionary draw could not be accommodated in the same year that the ERP overrun consumes the headroom, without displacing commitments already in flight. The AI Customer Service Chatbot was rejected for FY2027 on a combination of factors: its benefit case leaned on adoption-dependent deflection assumptions the committee judged optimistic in light of the POS omnichannel shortfall; its value would be materially higher sequenced after the CDP go-live rather than ahead of it; and it was the weakest candidate for scarce headroom precisely because it was the most discretionary and the most adoption-contingent, notwithstanding its attractive 33% headline IRR.

It is worth stating plainly that the year's constraint is being met from a position of underlying strength, not distress. The Cloud Infrastructure Migration is delivering: opex-savings realization reached approximately 97% of its $7.1M annual target by September, up from 95% at mid-year and 92% at the January PIR. That is precisely the kind of realized benefit that funds the company's continued capacity to invest, and it is the reason the constraint is a matter of sequencing within a single year rather than a retreat from the technology agenda.

I want to be equally plain about what the constraint is not. It is not a signal that the portfolio is over-committed in aggregate or that the December Board budget should contract the technology envelope. At an EBITDA margin around 9.1% on a $1.92B revenue base, the company's capacity to fund technology is real but finite, and the discipline the Steering Committee applied in September — funding commitments first and declining or deferring discretionary additions — is exactly the discipline that keeps that capacity intact. The ERP overrun is a bounded, one-program event whose additional cost buys the completion of a program the Board already ratified; it is not a recurring drag on the run-rate. Once ERP completes and its revised cost is absorbed, the FY2028 headroom that reopens is precisely what makes the WMS deferral to Q2 2027 a credible timing decision rather than a polite rejection. The prioritization below should therefore be read as a one-year sequencing plan, not as a permanent reordering of the company's technology ambitions.

A note on how the tiers were set is warranted, because the committee will reasonably ask why two sound cases were treated so differently. The distinguishing test was not headline return — on IRR alone the declined Chatbot at 33% and the deferred WMS at 22% both screen well, and the Chatbot in fact screens best of any case. The test was the combination of commitment status, benefit durability, and sequencing dependency. Committed, in-flight programs whose partial completion would be value-destroying rank first regardless of return. Among the genuinely new asks, a program whose benefits are durable and largely within management's control ranks above one whose benefits depend on adoption assumptions the organization has recently failed to meet, and a program that stands on its own ranks above one whose value is contingent on a foundation not yet in place. Applying that test, the ERP and Cybersecurity commitments and the near-complete CDP sit above the WMS pilot, and the WMS pilot — sound but discretionary and displaceable — sits above the Chatbot, whose adoption-dependence and sequencing dependency on the CDP placed it last.

## Recommended FY2027 Allocation

The recommended FY2027 allocation follows directly from the tiering.

First, fund the ERP Replacement to completion, including the revised scope quantified in the forthcoming change-request addendum. This is the single largest and least discretionary claim on FY2027 capital, and it must be resourced to finish; a half-completed ERP cutover is the worst outcome available to the company, both financially and operationally, given the number of systems that depend on it as the system of record.

Second, fund Cybersecurity Phase 2 to completion. Phase 1 is live and Phase 2 — the fleet-wide extension across the store and DC estate and the retirement of the legacy VPN concentrators — is in progress; completing it is what converts the program from a partial control into the full posture the compliance regime and cyber-insurance market now expect, and its $5.1M NPV is the highest in the portfolio.

Third, advance the CDP to its Q1 2027 go-live. The program is approved, funded, and close; the FY2027 ask is the incremental cost to carry it across the line, and its go-live also strengthens the eventual case for a customer-facing conversational capability that was declined this year partly on sequencing grounds.

Fourth, hold the WMS + Robotics Pilot for reconsideration at the Q2 2027 Steering Committee, refreshed for any changes in vendor pricing or distribution-center operating conditions. The deferral is a timing decision, and the program should return to the table as FY2027 capital visibility improves and the ERP revision is fully absorbed.

Fifth, take no FY2027 action on the AI Customer Service Chatbot. It is declined for FY2027; a future resubmission on a stronger foundation, particularly after the CDP is live and its deflection assumptions can be tested against unified customer data, remains open but is not a FY2027 commitment.

The two programs already in post-implementation monitoring — Cloud Infrastructure Migration and Omnichannel POS — carry no new FY2027 capital ask beyond the POS training-completion remediation already underway, and continue under the standard benefits-tracking cadence.

## Closing Recommendation and Ask

The recommendation is to adopt the FY2027 prioritization set out above: protect and complete the ERP and Cybersecurity commitments, advance the CDP to go-live, defer the WMS + Robotics Pilot to Q2 2027, and decline the AI Customer Service Chatbot for FY2027. This prioritization funds what the company is committed to and what the compliance and competitive environment require, sequences the discretionary agenda to the years in which it can be resourced properly, and does so from a portfolio that is demonstrably realizing benefit on its completed investments.

The specific ask of this memo is twofold. First, I ask the Steering Committee to endorse this FY2027 prioritization as the basis for the December budget submission. Second, I ask the Board Finance & Audit Committee, chaired by Harold Whitcombe, to note the forthcoming ERP change-request addendum and to be prepared to consider its ratification should the revised single-fiscal-year capital ask cross the $5M threshold, so that the ERP completion on which the FY2027 plan depends can proceed without a governance gap. I will bring the consolidated FY2027 allocation, incorporating the finalized ERP revision, to the December Steering Committee meeting for confirmation ahead of Board budget approval.
